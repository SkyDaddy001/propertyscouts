# NRI & Foreign Investor Real Estate Rules & Pain Points in India

---

## 1. GOVERNMENT RULES & REGULATIONS FOR NRI INVESTORS

### Property Types Allowed

✅ **CAN BUY:**
- Residential apartments (unlimited number)
- Commercial properties (offices, shops)
- Plots for construction (if within approved township/residential area)

❌ **CANNOT BUY:**
- Agricultural land
- Farmhouse/farm property
- Plantation property
- Rural property (unless inherited or gifted)

**Exception:** Can own agricultural/farmhouse land ONLY if inherited or gifted by an Indian relative.

---

### FEMA Compliance (Foreign Exchange Management Act)

**All NRI property purchases must comply with FEMA rules:**

1. **Source of Funds:** Must come from:
   - Remittances through banking channels (not cash/forex notes)
   - NRE accounts (Non-Resident External)
   - FCNR(B) accounts (Foreign Currency Non-Resident Bank)
   - NRO accounts (Non-Resident Ordinary)

2. **Payment Method:** ONLY through banking channels
   - ❌ NO traveler's cheques
   - ❌ NO foreign currency notes
   - ❌ NO cash payments
   - ✅ Bank transfer, demand draft, or credit facility ONLY

3. **Proof Required:**
   - Passport
   - Visa (visit visa, work visa, permanent resident visa)
   - Overseas address proof
   - Income/employment certificate from overseas
   - Bank statements showing fund remittance

## 2. TAXATION FOR NRI PROPERTY INVESTORS

### Rental Income Tax

**Applicable to:** Income from renting out your property in India

- **Taxable in India:** Even if you're non-resident, rental income is taxable
- **Tax Rate:** 30% slab (plus surcharge and cess)
- **Must File:** Annual ITR (Income Tax Return) in India
- **Deductible Expenses:** Property tax, maintenance, insurance, depreciation (can reduce taxable income)

---

### Capital Gains Tax (When Selling Property)

**Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG):** Property held < 2 years
- **Tax Rate:** 20% (as of 2024-25)
- **Indexed Cost:** NOT available (removed in July 2024)

**Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG):** Property held ≥ 2 years
- **Tax Rate:** 20% (with indexation benefit on cost)
- **Exemptions Available:** Section 54 (residential property), 54F, 54EC

**Example:**
- Buy property: ₹50 lakh
- Sell after 3 years: ₹65 lakh
- Long-term capital gain: ₹15 lakh
- Capital gains tax (20%): ₹3 lakh
- Net proceeds: ₹62 lakh

---

### TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) on Property Sales

**How it Works:**
- Buyer is required to deduct TDS on the purchase price
- TDS Rate: 5% (buyer deducts on full sale value, not profit)
- Buyer remits TDS to income tax department

**Pain Point:** TDS is deducted on FULL sale price, not profit
- Example: You sell for ₹1 crore
- Buyer deducts TDS: ₹5 lakh (5% of ₹1 crore)
- But you may owe tax on only ₹10 lakh profit (₹2 lakh tax)
- **You can claim ₹3 lakh refund** by filing ITR with Form 16A

**How to Reduce TDS:**
- Apply for **Lower Deduction Certificate (LDC)** using Form 13 under Section 197
- Requires proof of lower tax liability
- Buyer then deducts TDS on lower amount

## 3. HOME LOANS FOR NRI INVESTORS

### Eligibility
- ✅ Indian citizen OR OCI/PIO card holder
- ✅ Age: 21-65 years
- ✅ Stable overseas income (employment or business)
- ✅ Good credit score (700+)

### Loan Terms
- **LTV (Loan-to-Value):** 75-90% (varies by bank)
- **Interest Rate:** 7.5-8.5% p.a. (usually 0.5% higher than resident rates)
- **Tenure:** 15-20 years
- **Processing Fee:** 0.5-1% of loan amount

### Example
- Property cost: ₹60 lakh
- Down payment (20%): ₹12 lakh
- Loan amount (80%): ₹48 lakh
- EMI at 8% for 20 years: ₹39,957/month

## 4. REMITTANCE & REPATRIATION RULES

### How to Get Funds Out of India

**Allowed Methods:**
1. **LRS (Liberalized Remittance Scheme):** ₹250,000 (≈$3000) per financial year per person
2. **Sale Proceeds:** After deducting taxes, can repatriate through banking channels
3. **Rental Income:** Can accumulate in NRO account, then repatriate in blocks

**Process:**
- File ITR in India
- Get tax clearance certificate
- Bank transfers abroad
- Takes 3-7 business days

**Common Issue:** Attempting to repatriate ₹50 lakh at once when LRS limit is ₹2.5 lakh/year can trigger scrutiny.

## 7. INVESTMENT BENEFITS FOR NRI (How to Market)

### Tax-Optimized Benefits

1. **Capital Gains Exemption (Section 54):**
   - If you reinvest sale proceeds in another residential property within 6 months
   - Can exempt up to the cost of new property from capital gains tax

2. **Depreciation Deduction:**
   - 40% depreciation allowed in first 4 years on cost
   - Example: ₹1 crore property = ₹40 lakh depreciation in Year 1
   - Reduces taxable rental income

3. **Maintenance & Repair Deduction:**
   - 100% deduction on maintenance charges
   - Property taxes, insurance, tenant vetting costs all deductible

4. **No Long-Term Capital Gains Tax on Inherited Property:**
   - If parent gifted property to NRI child, step-up in cost basis eliminates prior gains

---

### Investment Appreciation Benefits

- **Fastest appreciating micro-markets:** Tambaram, Kattupakkam (Chennai)
