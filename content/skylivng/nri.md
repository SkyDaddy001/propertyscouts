## Tax Implications for NRI Owners

### India Tax on NRI Rental Income

**Annual Rental Income Filing:**
- ₹55K monthly = ₹6.6L annual
- File Indian Income Tax Return (ITR)
- Standard deduction: 30% of rental income (₹1.98L auto-deduction)
- Taxable income: ₹4.62L
- Tax at 30% bracket: ~₹1.39L
- **Net rental income: ₹5.21L (after India tax)**

**Key Points:**
- NRIs on foreign salary pay India tax only on Indian source income
- Foreign rental remains subject to Indian tax (if from India property)
- Depreciation benefits available (5% per year)
- Home loan interest deduction available (₹2L cap)

### Your Home Country Tax (USA, UAE, Singapore, etc.)

**USA NRI (Example):**
- Report foreign rental income on USA tax return
- Apply foreign earned income exclusion if applicable
- May get credit for India taxes paid
- Net result: Likely similar total tax to India rate

**UAE NRI (Example):**
- ZERO income tax in UAE on foreign income
- BUT must pay India tax
- No additional UAE tax liability
- Best tax position for Gulf workers

**Singapore NRI (Example):**
- Singapore taxes worldwide income
- Foreign property rental subject to SG tax
- Can claim credit for India taxes paid
- Consult SG accountant for exact position

**Recommendation:** Each NRI should consult tax accountant in home country before purchasing.
