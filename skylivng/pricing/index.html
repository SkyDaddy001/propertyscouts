Payment Plans: Flexibility Built In

Plan 1: Standard 20-Year Home Loan (Most Popular)

How It Works:

  1. Booking: ₹5-10L deposit (gets you the unit)
  2. Construction Phase: 50% payment (12-18 months)
  3. Possession: Remaining balance via home loan
  4. EMI: Monthly payments over 20 years

Best For:

  • Salaried professionals with stable income
  • Those wanting to build equity over time
  • Lower monthly commitment
  • Tax benefits (interest deduction)

Timeline:

  • Month 0: Book unit, pay ₹5-10L
  • Month 12-18: Construction phase, pay ₹26-31L
  • Month 24: Take possession, start ₹41K EMI
  • Year 20: Own your home free & clear

Plan 2: Extended 25-Year Loan (Lower Monthly Payment)

How It Works:

  • Same as 20-year, but spread over 25 years
  • EMI reduces by ₹5-7K/month
  • Additional interest paid (~₹5-8L more total)

Comparison:

  • 20-year @ 8%: ₹41,000/month
  • 25-year @ 8%: ₹36,000/month
  • Difference: ₹5,000/month lower payment

Best For:

  • Those wanting maximum monthly flexibility
  • Self-employed with variable income
  • Prefer lower obligation, comfortable with longer timeline

Plan 3: Accelerated 15-Year Loan (Quicker Ownership)

How It Works:

  • Pay off faster with higher monthly EMI
  • Significantly less total interest paid
  • Own free & clear in 15 years instead of 20

Comparison:

  • 20-year @ 8%: ₹41,000/month, ₹39.2L interest
  • 15-year @ 8%: ₹47,000/month, ₹30L interest
  • Saves: ₹9L interest, own 5 years earlier

Best For:

  • High-earning professionals
  • Those who want to own early
  • Priority: Minimal total interest payment

Plan 4: Bulk Payment (10-15 Years, Save ₹10L+)

How It Works:

  • Pay ₹75% upfront (gets you ₹10% discount on interest)
  • Finance only ₹25% via home loan
  • Own home much faster with lower interest

Example: ₹62L Property

  • Pay ₹46.5L upfront
  • Finance ₹15.5L only
  • EMI: ₹12,800/month for 15 years
  • Saves: ₹20L in interest + own early

Best For:

  • NRI investors with foreign funds
  • Business owners with cash reserves
  • Those prioritizing ownership over cash flow

Tax Benefits: Government Supports Your Purchase

Home Loan Interest Deduction (Section 24)

Annual Deduction: ₹2L maximum on home loan interest

Calculation Example (₹62L property, 8% loan):

  • Total interest in Year 1: ₹39.8K
  • Government allows you to deduct: ₹39.8K (all of it)
  • Tax saved: ₹39.8K × 30% = ₹12K/year
  • Effective loan cost drops to 7.7% (vs 8% nominal)

Over 20 Years:

  • Total interest paid: ₹39.2L
  • Tax savings (₹2L cap/year applies): ₹12L-15L lifetime
  • Net interest cost to you: ₹24-27L (not ₹39L)

Principal Repayment (No Direct Tax, But Wealth Building)

Every ₹41K EMI:

  • ₹32.7K goes to loan principal (building your equity)
  • ₹8.3K goes to interest (tax deductible ₹2L cap applies)

Wealth Building Over Time:

  • Year 1: Own 3% of property
  • Year 5: Own 25% of property
  • Year 10: Own 50% of property (half-way!)
  • Year 20: Own 100% (property is yours free & clear)

First-Time Home Buyer Benefits

Additional Tax Benefits:

  • Extra deduction available under Section 80EE (if applicable)
  • ₹50K additional interest deduction (in certain cases)
  • Stamp duty benefits vary by state

Check with CA: State-specific benefits apply
